# Executive Summary
Prepared a comprehensive research report on the empirical evaluation of virtue ethics in AI systems and experimental design. The literature review covered virtue‑based AI ethics frameworks (e.g., Hagendorff 2022) and critiques of principle‑only approaches, identifying proposed basic AI virtues (justice, honesty, responsibility, care) and second‑order virtues (prudence, fortitude). Empirical findings highlight the limited impact of principle knowledge on behavior and the role of cognitive resources in ethical decision‑making.

# Detailed Analysis by Themes
## Virtue‑Based Frameworks
- Definition of four basic AI virtues and two second‑order virtues that help mitigate bounded ethicality.
- Argument that virtue cultivation can complement principle‑based initiatives.

## Limitations of Principle‑Based Approaches
- Empirical research shows plain knowledge of ethics does not measurably influence decision‑making.
- Ethical decision‑making requires cognitive energy; virtues like prudence support the shift from intuitive to reflective reasoning.

## Experimental Design Recommendations
- Use mixed‑methods, organizational‑level interventions, and validated psychometric measures of virtues.
- Employ behavioral outcome measures (decision‑making tasks, simulated development tasks).
- Conduct randomized controlled trials comparing virtue‑training with principle‑based training, longitudinal field studies to observe value‑action gap closure, and ensure ecological validity in industrial settings.

## Quantitative Evidence
- References to empirical moral psychology studies (e.g., Mead et al. 2009 on self‑control depletion) indicating cognitive constraints on ethical behavior.
- Meta‑analyses suggesting principle‑based interventions often fail to change behavior.

# Implications and Future Outlook
- Recommend compiling a systematic literature review and designing experimental protocols (RCTs of virtue‑training vs. principle‑based training).
- Develop measurement instruments (virtue scales, behavioral tasks) and pilot them in industry with pre‑registered analysis plans.
- Anticipate that integrating virtue ethics could improve ethical decision‑making robustness and address bounded ethicality in AI development.
